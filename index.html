Bill Gates and Pancakes

Gates' Research

Now, let me tell you a little something about a guy called Bill Gates. Many people know him as the guy who started Microsoft. But not many people know about his passion for pancake flipping. He is so into it that he even authored a research paper for an algorithm for optimal pancake flipping. The problem that he attempted, as described in the paper, was:

The chef in our place is sloppy, and when he prepares to deliver a stack of pancakes they come out all different sizes. Therefore, when I deliver them to a customer, on the way to the table I rearrange them (so that the smallest winds up on top, and so on, down to the largest at the bottom) by grabbing several from the top and flipping them over, repeating this (varying the number I flip) as many times as necessary. If there are n pancakes, what is the maximum number of flips (as a function f(n) of n) that I will ever have to use to rearrange them?

I found his paper, weirdly enough in the archives of UCB, even though he never studied there.
